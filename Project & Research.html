An Assessment of the Water Quality Index of the Dhaleshwari and Turag Rivers

A comprehensive environmental engineering research project analyzing water pollution levels and quality parameters in two major rivers of Bangladesh using advanced laboratory techniques and WQI methodology.

19 Parameters Tested 4 Sampling Points March 2021 - March 2022

Project Overview

Research Objective

This comprehensive study focuses on evaluating the surface water quality of two major rivers in Bangladesh - the Turag and Dhaleshwari rivers. The research aims to determine pollution levels through systematic analysis of physicochemical parameters and calculate Water Quality Index (WQI) values to assess water suitability for various uses.

Key Research Questions

  • What are the current pollution levels in Turag and Dhaleshwari rivers?
  • How do upstream and downstream water quality parameters compare?
  • What are the main sources of contamination affecting these water bodies?
  • Is the water suitable for domestic, agricultural, or industrial use?

WQI Calculation Method

Weighted Arithmetic Index Method:

WQI = Σ(Qᵢ × Wᵢ) / ΣWᵢ

Where:

  • Qᵢ: Quality rating scale
  • Wᵢ: Relative weight (1/Sᵢ)
  • Sᵢ: Standard permissible value
  • Vₙ: Actual parameter value
  • Vᵢ: Ideal parameter value
WQI Classification
0-25Excellent
26-50Good
51-75Poor
76-100Very Poor
>100Unfit

Critical Parameters Exceeding Standards

Turag River Issues:
  • Total Suspended Solids: 101-104 mg/L (Standard: 10 mg/L)
  • Turbidity: 39-45 NTU (Standard: 10 NTU)
  • Color: 27-29 Pt-Co (Standard: 15 Pt-Co)
  • COD: 10-11 mg/L (Standard: 4 mg/L)
  • Ammonia (Upstream): 1.19 mg/L (Standard: 0.5 mg/L)
Dhaleshwari River Issues:
  • Total Suspended Solids: 143-177 mg/L (Standard: 10 mg/L)
  • Color: 33-42 Pt-Co (Standard: 15 Pt-Co)
  • COD: 7-9 mg/L (Standard: 4 mg/L)
  • Ammonia: 1.37-2.42 mg/L (Standard: 0.5 mg/L)

Parameters Within Standards

Acceptable Levels Found:
  • pH: 7.34-7.58 (Standard: 6.5-8.5)
  • Heavy Metals: All within safe limits
  • Hydrogen Sulfide: Below detection limits
  • Electrical Conductivity: Within acceptable range
  • Chloride Content: Normal levels detected

Key Insights

  • Both rivers show "Very Poor" water quality (WQI 76-100)
  • Water unsuitable for drinking without treatment
  • Industrial pollution is the primary concern
  • Dissolved oxygen critically low (0.3-0.56 mg/L)
  • Aquatic life severely threatened
